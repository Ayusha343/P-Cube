# Design and Components

## 5.1 System Architecture

The P Cube system consists of several integrated subsystems that work in harmony to deliver electric-assisted transportation:

**Figure 1 – P Cube System Architecture Overview**

- **Motor and Power Transmission System**: Converts electrical energy into mechanical motion through a geared motor arrangement
- **Energy Storage System**: Sealed lead-acid batteries configured to provide 24V power
- **Control and Monitoring System**: Speed throttle, voltage monitoring, and hybrid mode functionality
- **Lighting and Safety System**: Headlight, brake light, and key-lock security
- **Support Structure**: Mechanical mounting framework and protective enclosures

## 5.2 Component Sourcing Philosophy

Rather than manufacturing components from raw materials, the P Cube project emphasizes a sustainable approach through:

- **Recycled Materials**: Components salvaged from discarded electronics and vehicles
- **Cost Optimization**: Comparison shopping across online and offline vendors
- **Quality Verification**: Testing compatibility and durability before assembly
- **Minimal Waste**: Reusing functional components instead of purchasing new alternatives

## 5.3 ATL Tools and Resources

The Atal Tinkering Lab (ATL) at school provided critical resources that would otherwise have been inaccessible for a student-led project. Access to ATL tools and components significantly reduced the cost of fabrication and enabled precision work that home tools could not achieve.

**Tools Used from ATL**: Drill, saw, soldering iron, heat gun

**Components Sourced from ATL**: Screws, switches, LEDs, resistors, capacitors, and electrical connectors

The soldering iron and heat gun from ATL were particularly valuable for assembling and weatherproofing the electrical system. The drill enabled precise mounting holes in the aluminium bracket, and the saw was used for cutting and shaping structural components.

ATL components such as switches and connectors were used in the control panel and wiring harness, further keeping costs within the project's budget.
