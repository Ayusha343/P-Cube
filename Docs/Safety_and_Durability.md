# Safety and Durability

## 10.1 Electrical Safety

The system operates at 24V DC, which presents minimal electrical safety hazards. This voltage level is:

- Below human body electrocution thresholds
- Commonly used in RVs, boats, and solar systems
- Intrinsically safe for wet environments
- Capable of being handled directly without risk in emergency situations

## 10.2 Weather Protection

All electrical components are housed in an acrylic protective case with rubber sealing. The bicycle has been left parked outdoors at school in various weather conditions and has been washed with water multiple times. The electrical system has never experienced failures related to water exposure or corrosion.

## 10.3 Mechanical Durability

- Steel sprocket and freewheel withstand high torque
- Sealed ball bearings resist corrosion
- Recycled rubber provides vibration isolation
- Epoxy bonding maintains component integrity

## 10.4 Safety Features

**Key-Lock Security** provides immediate power cutoff when key is removed, prevention of unauthorized operation, no complex electronics to fail, and compliance with transport safety standards.

**Braking System**: The electric bicycle retains the original bicycle braking system. Because the motor is not regenerative, it does not interfere with braking. Brake performance remains identical to the original bicycle.

## 10.5 Thermal Management

The 250W motor generates moderate heat during operation. The bicycle's natural movement provides passive cooling through air circulation. Sealed lead-acid batteries have built-in thermal management and will not enter thermal runaway conditions during normal charging—a significant safety advantage over lithium systems.
