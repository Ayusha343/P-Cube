# Performance Metrics

After approximately two years of consistent use, including daily commuting, the P Cube has demonstrated reliable performance. The following metrics were measured under controlled conditions.

##  Speed Performance

| Mode | Maximum Speed |
| --- | --- |
| Pedal Only (No Motor) | ~23 km/h (depends on rider fitness) |
| Motor Only (No Pedaling) | ~25 km/h (limited by motor RPM and gearing) |
| Hybrid Mode (Pedal + Motor) | ~45 km/h (combined torque boost) |

**Note**: The 45 km/h hybrid mode speed represents the theoretical maximum under ideal conditions. In practice, this speed is rarely used due to safety considerations and bicycle braking limitations.

## Climbing and Acceleration Performance

When riding with friends on standard bicycles, the P Cube consistently outperformed conventional bicycles even when operating the motor at only 10–20% power. The mechanical advantage provided by the 1:8 gear ratio enables climbing steep inclines with relative ease.

Motor torque multiplication (0.8 Nm base to 6.4 Nm effective) provides sufficient force for most urban topography.

##  Range and Endurance

| Mode | Approximate Range |
| --- | --- |
| Motor Only (24V, 7Ah battery) | 7–10 km at moderate throttle |
| Hybrid Mode (Pedal + Motor) | 15+ km (human power supplements battery) |

Range varies with terrain, throttle level, battery age, and rider load.

##  Reliability Observations

- **Motor operation**: Continuous and reliable with no mechanical failures
- **Power transmission**: Gearing and chain system stable after initial tensioning
- **Frame and structure**: No fatigue cracks or structural degradation
- **Mounting system**: Remained secure throughout the two-year testing period

##  Control Responsiveness

The handlebar-mounted throttle provides smooth, proportional speed control. Acceleration is responsive and smooth, enabling precise maneuvering in urban environments. The freewheel design prevents motor back-drive when pedaling, which simplifies the system design but foregoes regenerative braking.
