# Conclusion

The P Cube – Power Pedal Pro represents a successful case study in practical engineering education, sustainable innovation, and problem-solving. What began as a personal project during a school break evolved into a refined, tested system that demonstrates both technical competence and creative thinking.

## Key Takeaways

- Affordability is achievable through intelligent component selection and resourceful sourcing
- Hands-on learning through troubleshooting and iteration leads to superior engineering intuition
- Sustainability and economics are not contradictory—recycled materials reduce both cost and environmental impact
- Creative problem-solving (plug socket switching, epoxy bonding) can substitute for expensive off-the-shelf components
- Failure and iteration are essential parts of the engineering process
- Real-world testing reveals insights that theoretical design cannot provide

The P Cube project has equipped me with practical engineering skills, deep understanding of mechanical and electrical systems integration, and confidence to tackle complex technical challenges. The project showcases the potential for engineering education to create tangible, marketable solutions while maintaining a focus on sustainability and social impact. Whether as a personal portfolio piece, a proof-of-concept for affordable e-mobility, or a springboard for future innovation, the P Cube demonstrates that meaningful engineering can emerge from limited resources, high motivation, and persistent problem-solving.
